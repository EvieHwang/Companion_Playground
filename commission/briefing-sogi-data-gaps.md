
# SOGI Data

As I understand it, Washington State can’t fully verify whether it is serving LGBTQ+ residents on the same terms as everyone else in foster care, adult homelessness, Medicaid, unemployment, or the basic-needs safety net because the agencies running those systems do not sufficiently collect, share and use  sexual orientation and gender identity (SOGI) information about their clients.

National research consistently finds LGBTQ+ people over-represented in foster care, homelessness, behavioral health crises, and economic insecurity, so it is of interest and concern to the community that we support our State’s migration onto data systems that provide for this need. 

Note that privacy and security of data is not a reason to avoid doing this work - this is a design problem with commonly available solutions. Oregon state has already demonstrated that confidentiality and statewide collection are compatible.

I was able to find examples where Washington has made real progress in this area:
- **Hospitals report SOGI** in patient discharge data (since 2023).
- **The Healthy Youth Survey** gives Washington one of the strongest data sources in the country on LGBTQ+ youth health and well-being.
- **The state's BRFSS health survey** includes SOGI questions, supporting population-level health estimates.
- **Apple Health** maintains records for trans members receiving gender-affirming care.
- **The Office of Equity** has statutory authority to set standards for disaggregated data collection.

While each of these is a genuine accomplishment, the problem is that they were built one at a time, in different statutes, with different question sets, and they leave large gaps.

Systems where I was not able to determine that sufficient data was being captured were:
- **DCYF FamLink** - Whether LGBTQ+ youth are over-represented in foster care, and how their placements compare
- **Balance-of-State HMIS** - How LGBTQ+ adults experience homelessness and shelter access
- **Apple Health intake** - Whether LGBTQ+ Washingtonians face barriers getting and keeping coverage
- **ESD Unemployment Insurance** - Whether LGBTQ+ workers face differential outcomes in the labor market
- **DSHS ESA intake** - TANF, Basic Food, ABD | LGBTQ+ household economic vulnerability in the safety net

###  Proposed Commission Goals

I would like the Commission to champion this issue and work within the government to make meaningful progress over the next year. Here are three ways we might take this work on:

**1. Advocate for SOGI system-by-system** We could meet with department leadership to advocate for this approach, such as with the Department of Children, Youth, and Families to encourage usage of SOGI data in FamLink.

**2. Request Office of Equity to publish a data standard.** The Office of Equity already has the legal authority to publish a binding statewide data standard. This achieves question-set unification without new legislation.

**3. Washington equivalent of Oregon's HB 3159.** Draft and seek sponsorships for a statute creating a centralized registry, a unified standard, binding requirements across state-funded programs, and dedicated funding.

### Appendix: What the questions actually look like

A reasonable concern is that SOGI data collection could become an unwieldy list of identity categories. Serious standard-setters have already worked this out. Every credible standard uses a small closed list of categories — common enough to support statistical analysis — paired with an "I use a different term [specify]" write-in field that captures any identity not on the list. The list is finite and revisable, not exhaustive.

**Federal Interagency Working Group recommendation (OMB, January 2023):**

- Sexual orientation: Gay or lesbian; Straight, that is not gay or lesbian; Bisexual; "I use a different term [specify]." Plus Don't know and Prefer not to answer.
- Gender identity uses a two-step method:
  - Sex assigned at birth: Female; Male.
  - Current gender: Female; Male; Transgender; "I use a different term [specify]." Plus Don't know and Prefer not to answer.

**Oregon REALD+SOGI standard (permanent rules adopted July 2024):**

- Sexual orientation: Asexual; Bisexual/pansexual; Gay; Lesbian; Queer; Straight/heterosexual; Same-gender loving; "Something else fits better [specify]"; Don't know; Don't want to answer.
- Gender identity (check all that apply): Agender; Androgynous; Genderqueer/gender non-conforming/gender fluid; Man/boy; Non-binary; Questioning; Trans/transgender; Two-Spirit; Woman/girl; "Something else fits better [specify]"; Don't know; Don't want to answer.
- Sex assigned at birth (separate question): Female; Male; X; Don't know; Don't want to answer.

Oregon's list sits at the longer end of national practice. Identities outside that list are recorded through the write-in field rather than enumerated as checkboxes.
