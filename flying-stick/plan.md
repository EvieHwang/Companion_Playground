# Flying Stick

## Why

Flying Stick is my development practice. The goal is fluency, not a portfolio.

I build because the world has too much friction in the things that matter — food, housing, healthcare — and I cannot ignore it. The instinct is upstream: clean the signal at the point of entry, not at the dashboard. Supply chain logic, scaled to human conditions.

I always feel unfinished. I think that is correct, because the world is unfinished. The compulsion is real and I do not need to apologize for it. It is worth aiming.

I am still learning. Most of this is training, not performing — 90% training, 10% competing. The idea behind each build is good enough to make the test valid. The deliverable is fluency, not the artifact.

## The working hypothesis

The deep claim worth testing across the portfolio: meta-building is doing real lever work on friction elimination, even when individual builds get eaten.

I build infrastructure for building. Each build is a small lever on someone else’s throughput — mine first, eventually others’. The garden gets closer when more people are building well, not only when I am. The Doozer compulsion is doing real work — or it is self-pleasing motion. The plan tests which.

## Operating mode

A build qualifies as valid training when the idea is real enough that the test surfaces actual judgment calls, when I can name what the exercise is training before I start, and when I can name what would tell me the training is enough.

A build qualifies as performing when something I have built is ready for use beyond me. Right now: Markus, DWCOA. The rest is training.
