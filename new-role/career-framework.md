# Eve Hwang — My Career Framework

## What I’m solving for

I’m a value stream leader. I’m looking for a Director+ role in operational flow systems at a well-run company that conducts itself with integrity. I work at the intersection of conceptual rigor and operational reality, and I lead diverse cross-functional teams through prioritization, coaching, and structural clarity. My next role steps me up from Senior PM into formal leadership with direct reports (or a directly-led cross-functional team), real scope, and structural protection for my work.

## Role shape

- **Level:** Director+ or equivalent. Director of Operations, Director of Fulfillment, Director of Programs, Head of Product, Head of Operations, GM, Chief of Staff at scale, or VP of a function with cross-functional scope.
- **Authority:** Prioritization. I tell teams what matters and align priorities with value. I don’t tell them how to do it. That’s enough.
- **Team:** Diverse and cross-functional, whether they report to me directly (program managers, analysts, ops leads, mixed) or one abstraction away (managing PMs who each lead their own cross-functional teams). Either works; same craft.
- **Three defining characteristics — all three required:** cross-functional scope, prioritization authority, clear outcome accountability. Any role missing one of those three is the wrong job, regardless of title.

## Domain thesis

I work in operational flow systems where a rigorous logical model maps to physical or operational reality, and the maintenance of that connection is the work.

The underlying craft is the same across domains: donors → gifts → campaigns → revenue. SKUs → cases → eaches → pallets. Tickets → incidents → changes → service health. Priorities → programs → resources → outcomes. I’m a systems-of-flow person.

**In scope:** Supply chain, fulfillment, logistics, e-commerce operations, marketplace operations, returns and reverse logistics, fundraising operations at scale, healthcare operations (claims, inventory, patient flow). Anything with high transaction volume and an integration story.

**Out of scope:** Pure AI/ML product companies where the substance is the algorithm — at this career stage, I’m not learning a new domain from scratch. AI as a tool inside operational systems is welcome; AI as the thing the company makes is not my fight. Also out: single-function leadership, pure strategy/consulting without operational ownership.

## Floor — non-negotiable

These are not preferences. They are filters. Companies that fail them are excluded before any other criterion applies.

1. **Integrity in conduct.** The company is doing something I can stand behind without flinching.
1. **Healthy treatment of frontline workers.** The company’s success is not built on people getting hurt.

What this eliminates: fast-fashion supply chains, warehouse-injury-prone retailers, gig platforms that misclassify workers, pharma with opioid history, financial services with predatory products, companies in PE squeezes that gut headcount, gambling, short-term lending, 3PLs whose competitive edge is labor cost. It pulls me toward operationally complex companies whose competitive edge is something other than worker exploitation.

## Preferences inside the floor, ordered

1. **Clear functional ownership.** Low matrix complexity. My scope is real and not constantly relitigated. (Highest weight.)
1. **Real performance culture.** Delivery matters more than performance theater.
1. **Operational maturity.** People execute, not just plan. The operating system actually works.
1. **Stable executive leadership.** Low direct weight — I don’t care about it intrinsically. But I treat it as an instrumental tell: if leadership churn is high, the other three rarely hold.

## Target archetypes — two clusters

**Tech-flavored operational platforms — preferred.** Faster-moving, digital-native, higher uncertainty, higher ceilings.
Examples: Flexport, ShipBob, Amazon (Director-level only), Microsoft Cloud Operations, Auger.

**Traditional or mission-coherent operations at scale — also viable.** Slower-moving, more mature, more stability, real operational depth.
Examples: Costco, Kaiser Permanente, REI, Patagonia, Williams Sonoma, RH, Crate & Barrel.

**Adjacent worth watching:** Member cooperatives like Land O’Lakes; healthcare operations at non-predatory players.

**Hard skips:** Boeing (labor and safety crisis), Amazon-adjacent (Whole Foods, Zappos — too close to what I just left), anything that fails the floor.

## Daily-texture model

I have two modes, and I want a role that has both.

**Setup mode** — when I join, or when I take on a new initiative. Intense, plan-heavy, organizing, motivating, clearing the field. I assess, build the mental model, motivate resources, get the work ahead of the game.

**Steady state** — what I’m in once setup is done. I monitor metrics. I spot breakdowns. I coach my people through them, helping them see what they missed, supporting them in taking new actions and learning new skills. This is where people transformation happens. It’s the deepest satisfaction of the work, and it’s what I want my days to look like.

The bad versions: chaos that prevents flow state, or maintenance with no breakdowns to spot. I need real complexity and at least some organizational maturity, but not so mature that everything’s locked down.

## My pattern of burn — and the protection my work requires

**The pattern.** I do substantive structural work that creates real value. Someone breaks it, claims it, deems it disposable, or holds me accountable for what they failed at. I escalate; leadership hedges, defers, or fails to protect me. I leave.

**The meta-pattern.** The work I love (structural, cross-functional, system-building) is exactly the kind of work most vulnerable to credit-taking, executive whim, and being deemed non-essential in re-orgs. I’ve been drawn to work that’s structurally underprotected, and I’ve kept landing in environments that don’t protect it. The agency move is to choose a place where my work is legible to power and protected by power.

**What protection looks like in practice:**

- Title and scope that make my work attributable to me by design (Director+ rather than Senior PM)
- An executive sponsor with the authority and intent to protect my scope
- Low-matrix organizational design — my scope isn’t constantly being relitigated by peers
- Stable leadership over my area (the instrumental tell)
- A culture where my contributions are legible (visible, attributable, recognized) rather than absorbed invisibly
